About the Technology

What is Low-Temperature Sludge Drying?

The Low-Temperature Sludge Dryer is the final stage in the sludge treatment chain, taking dewatered sludge cake (15–25% DS from a Belt Filter Press or Screw Press) and reducing residual moisture to achieve a granular or powdery product with up to 92% dry solids content.

By operating below 70°C using heat pumps or low-grade waste heat, these dryers achieve maximum energy efficiency while eliminating the risks associated with high-temperature systems (fires, explosions). The resulting dried sludge is a stable, lightweight product with dramatically reduced volume — ideal for landfill, co-incineration, or use as soil amendment.

  • Up to 92% DS — granular or powdery dried sludge product
  • Operation below 70°C — safe, no fire/explosion risk
  • Up to 80% volume reduction vs. dewatered cake
  • Compatible with waste heat — heat pumps, solar, CHP exhaust
  • Odor control — enclosed system with air treatment
  • Fully automated continuous 24h operation
Two Series Available

EAHD vs EAWD — Choosing the right series

EAHD Series

Heat Pump Dryer
  • Electrically-driven heat pump system
  • Self-contained — no external heat source required
  • Ideal where waste heat is unavailable
  • Operating temperature 45–70°C
  • COP > 3.5 — highly energy-efficient

EAWD Series

Waste Heat / Solar Dryer
  • Uses available low-grade waste heat
  • Compatible with biogas CHP, steam, solar
  • Lower OPEX where waste heat is available
  • Ideal for plants with digestion + CHP
  • Near-zero additional energy cost scenario
Operating Principle

How does Low-Temperature Sludge Drying work?

Cake Feeding

Dewatered sludge cake (15–25% DS) is fed continuously into the dryer by a conveyor or screw feeder.

Low-Temp Drying

Sludge is exposed to a gentle hot-air flow (below 70°C) in the drying chamber, evaporating residual moisture.

Vapor Condensation

Evaporated moisture is condensed in the heat exchanger and recovered as condensate for reuse or treatment.

Dry Product Discharge

Dry, granular or powdery sludge (up to 92% DS) discharged continuously for final disposal or valorization.
